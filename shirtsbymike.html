Redesign

Project

For this project, I was tasked to redesign shirtsbymike, an e-commerce t-shirt website for the Treehouse audience.

Problem

The website is dated and needs a refresh in branding to highlight a featured t-shirt.

Duration

November to December 2020.

Responsibilites

Sketch, wireframe, and mockup.

Old Website Critique

Design Principles

Color

On the landing page, the colors coral and baby green are complementary. However, the colors become dull when scrolling down the landing page.

Shapes

The use of rectangles help display each t-shirt individually.

Grouping

The shirts are grouped by "t-shirts" and "tank-tops." This grouping distinguishes each style of t-shirt and makes it easier to browse the collection.

User Flow

The user flow is logical, that is, the user browses the collection, selects a t-shirt, reviews the product details, adds it to the cart, and proceeds to checkout.

Assumptions

When thinking about redesigning the website, I made a few assumptions. First, I assumed users are interested in a visually appealing website. I assumed users are happy when they can easily find new products on the site, thus organization was important for the redesign. Finally, users are interested in sharing new products, so I linked the company's social media in the footer to let users view the latest social trends about the company.

Color Palette

I used mostly cool colors instead of warm colors to give a more inviting feeling to the site.

Typography

I used Baloo Chettan for headers because I thought it complements the fun and cute look of the company's mascot, Mike the frog.

Brand Personality

I designed the site to have a casual, fun, and inclusive personality that appeals to the target age demographic of users between the ages 16 and 35.

Text Content

I kept the content simple and descriptive and added a feature called "Hop Shop," which behaves like quick shop on the Disney website.
