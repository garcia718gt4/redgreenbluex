Project

Under the Google UX Design program, I designed a responsive website that guided the user to complete a volunteer registration flow for a charity.

Target Audience

The target audience was those who volunteer in their community at least once a month or plan to volunteer in the near future.

Goal

To determine if the user can easily navigate the website and register to volunteer without any prompting.

Duration

May to June, 2021

Responsibilites

Designed the responsive website from conception to delivery i.e., conducted interviews, created paper and digital wireframes, low and high-fidelity prototypes, conducted a usability study, and accounted for accessibility.

User Research Summary

Before I began ideating ideas for a user flow, I first conducted user interviews to understand the people I was designing for. Interviewees included adults between the ages of 21 and 38, and I asked them about their community involvement. The feedback I received from the interviews resulted in a problem statement for a user persona called Madison.

Problem Statement

Madison is a dental hygienist in a new city who needs a way to easily sign up to volunteer for a local charity because it's rewarding for her to serve her community.

Wireframes

To demonstrate a responsive website, I first included the homepage of the desktop version with its accompanying responsive version. Second, I included the volunteer page with its accompanying responsive screen. I placed labels where users can easily find them and navigate to register as a volunteer.

Research Plan

The main research goal was to understand if participants can easily navigate a user flow. I tested five participants individually in a moderated usability study. The majority of participants were alert and seemed to understand the directions. Each usability study session lasted about 10 minutes.

Study participants included:
4 women and 1 man with ages ranging from 21 to 38.
Finally, I recorded the session and completed a behavioral observation checklist.

Study Summary

Research revealed most participants were able to comfortably navigate the user flow. However, a common response amongst participants was if the volunteer button could also be found in the navigation bar or be nested in the navigation, as they reported they were familiar with this type of design. The insight that users want multiple ways to enter a user flow was key to creating the mockups. Overall, almost all participants reported they found the study easy and simple to complete.

Mockups

For the mockups, I incorporated user feedback and added a dropdown menu where the user can select "volunteer" instead of having to scroll down to the volunteer section. I left in place what users found comfortable and easy to use, mainly the large labels and dividers of the webpage. Finally, my goal for the mockup was to design a simple and appealing website that would not take long for a user to fill-out and submit an online registration form.

Accessibility Considerations

I made use of color contrast to enhance the readability of text against darker background colors. One way to increase the accessibility of the website in future testing is to include people who use switches or other assistive technologies.

Learnings

This was a fun project to work on. Given that I currently work for a non-profit serving homeless veterans, I took special care in designing how a non-profit website should look and feel. I learned that responsive web design should carry the same feeling of engagement, though not necessarily look, across multiple window or screen sizes.
